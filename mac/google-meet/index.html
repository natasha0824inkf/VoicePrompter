Frequently asked questions

How does voice scrolling work?
VoicePrompter uses Apple's native speech recognition to listen to what you say and match it in real time against your script. Everything happens entirely on your device - no audio is ever sent to any server, ensuring 100% privacy and security.
Will the teleprompter window show up on Google Meet screen sharing?
No. VoicePrompter uses macOS system-level exclusions. When sharing your screen or browser window on Google Meet, the prompter window remains completely invisible to other participants.
Which apps does it work with?
VoicePrompter works simultaneously with Google Meet and any other app on your Mac - conferencing apps, screen recorders, presentation tools, streaming software, anything. It runs at the system level and doesn't require any plugins or modifications.
Where do I store my scripts?
Wherever you already work - Google Docs, Apple Notes, Notion, Obsidian, or any other app. When you're ready, copy the text and click Load from Clipboard in the menu bar. The window opens instantly with your script loaded.
Are my scripts saved?
VoicePrompter automatically saves up to 100 recent scripts locally on your device so you can reload them with one click. Nothing is uploaded or synced anywhere.
Can I add stage directions or notes I don't want to read out loud?
Yes - put anything in square brackets and VoicePrompter will display it but skip it during voice scrolling. Useful for cues like [pause], [smile], or [check slide].
What if my script includes words in a different language?
Put those words in square brackets and the speech recognition algorithm will skip over them, so you can keep speaking in your primary language without the sync breaking.
Can I jump to a specific part of my script?
Yes - tap any word on screen and VoicePrompter starts listening from exactly that point. Useful if you skip a section, restart a segment, or want to jump ahead.
Will loud environments affect voice scrolling?
Background noise can interfere with speech recognition in extreme cases, but VoicePrompter handles noisy environments well and has been tested reliably in louder settings. Normal office noise, keyboard sounds, or other participants speaking on your call won't cause issues.
Does it work across multiple screens?
Yes - you can place the VoicePrompter window on any display. If you use an external monitor as a separate display, the window is visible only on whichever screen you place it on. If you mirror your screens, the window will appear on both - so for calls where you share your screen, use extend mode rather than mirror mode.
Is my data private?
VoicePrompter does not collect, process, or transmit your scripts or any other personal data. Everything runs on your device. This is verified by Apple - the app passed Apple's review process for security and data privacy before being approved on the Mac App Store.
Which Mac models are supported?
VoicePrompter works on every Mac running macOS 15 or later, including both Intel and Apple Silicon models.
Is the app free?
VoicePrompter is free to use with up to 3 custom scripts, each with unlimited words and unlimited sessions. A demo script is also available for free with no limits. If you need more than 3 scripts, you can upgrade to Pro monthly, yearly, or with a one-time lifetime purchase.
How do I read a script naturally during a video call?
To read naturally on a video call: 1. Position your teleprompter script as close as possible to your webcam (VoicePrompter lets you float the window directly next to your camera). 2. Use voice-activated scrolling so you don't need manual clickers or keyboards. 3. Adjust the window transparency and font size so you can read comfortably without losing eye contact with participants.
Do I need to install a Google Meet browser extension?
No. VoicePrompter is a native macOS app. It runs completely independently, so you don't need to install any Chrome extensions, Safari add-ons, or modify your browser settings to use it with Google Meet.
Can other Google Meet participants tell I am reading a script?
No. Since you can drag and resize the transparent VoicePrompter window directly next to or below your Mac's webcam, your eye line remains completely natural. Other participants will see you looking directly at them while you read.
How to use a teleprompter with Google Meet?
Using VoicePrompter with Google Meet is simple: 1. Launch VoicePrompter on your Mac. 2. Load your script. 3. Position the transparent prompter window directly next to your webcam. 4. Share your screen or browser window in Google Meet—the prompter is automatically excluded and invisible to other participants. 5. Speak naturally, and the prompter will scroll in sync with your voice.
